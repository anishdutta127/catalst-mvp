# PART 1: THE THREE STYLE DIRECTIONS

Before individual screens, we need to lock **one visual language** that carries across all 13 backgrounds. Here are three directions — each solves the Cedric-in-background, dark-centre, activity-integrated brief differently. Read all three, then I'll tell you which one to lock and why.

---

## STYLE A: "THE LIVING ATLAS"
### *Naturalist Editorial × Warm Botanical Realism*

**What it looks like:** Imagine the field journals of a Victorian botanist who discovered a magical garden — drawn with the precision of a National Geographic illustrator but lit by a Studio Ghibli colourist. Warm, inviting, approachable. Cedric feels like a character in a high-end nature documentary about a place that doesn't exist.

**Cedric treatment:** Rendered with soft painterly realism. White-robed sage with warm skin tones. Always interacting with the environment — touching a vine, reading a tome, adjusting something on a workbench. He looks like he *lives* here. Slightly soft focus when he's at the edges.

**Circuit-root motif:** Subtle. Visible only in root systems and stone carvings — like an archaeological detail you notice on second look. Rendered as delicate golden tracery.

**Centre zone:** Rich dark earth/soil/stone floor with faint bioluminescent veins at extreme edges. The centre reads as "ground level" — organic and warm. UI elements would sit like objects placed on a dark garden path.

**Mood keywords:** `warm botanical illustration, golden hour ambient light, naturalist journal aesthetic, soft painterly focus, Ghibli colour grading, cinematic depth of field, living garden interior`

**Who it appeals to:** People who love Ghibli, nature, wellness apps, Headspace/Calm aesthetic. Skews warm, approachable, feminine. Strong emotional pull.

**Risk:** Could feel too soft/whimsical for founders who want to feel like they're discovering something *powerful*. May read as "children's app" at thumbnail size.

---

## STYLE B: "THE VERDANIA CODEX"
### *Cinematic Concept Art × Prestige Dark Fantasy*

**What it looks like:** This is what a Denis Villeneuve film would look like if it was set in an enchanted startup garden. High contrast. Dramatic chiaroscuro lighting. Every frame looks like it was art-directed for a $200M film. The garden isn't cute — it's ancient, intelligent, and slightly imposing. The beauty is in the *atmosphere*, not the prettiness.

**Cedric treatment:** Rendered as cinematic concept art — detailed robes with texture, strong rim lighting, dramatic silhouette. He's a character you'd see in Dune or The Lord of the Rings concept gallery. Sometimes shown from behind or in three-quarter profile to avoid Midjourney face inconsistency. His white hair catches light dramatically. He has *presence*.

**Circuit-root motif:** Bold and structural. The roots look like a living circuit board — golden traces with node-points that pulse. It's the design signature. Visible in walls, floors, tree trunks. Rendered with the precision of a technical illustration merged into the organic material.

**Centre zone:** Deep atmospheric void — like looking into a dark cathedral nave. Volumetric haze/mist creates natural depth. The centre isn't "empty" — it has atmosphere, light rays, subtle particle depth. UI elements would float like holographic projections in a sacred space.

**Mood keywords:** `cinematic concept art, dramatic chiaroscuro lighting, prestige dark fantasy, volumetric atmospheric haze, golden bioluminescent circuit-root tracery, ancient intelligent architecture, Villeneuve colour grading, cathedral-scale composition`

**Who it appeals to:** Universal. Men AND women. Founders AND dreamers. People who love film, gaming, premium tech products. The "I've never seen anything like this" reaction. Reads as premium at every scale — from thumbnail to fullscreen.

**Risk:** Almost none. Cinematic atmosphere is the most universally appealing visual language because it works on a subconscious level (we've all been trained by film). The only risk is execution — Midjourney needs very precise prompting to hit this consistently.

---

## STYLE C: "THE DREAMING MACHINE"
### *Tech-Organic Surrealism × Digital Mysticism*

**What it looks like:** M.C. Escher meets Hayao Miyazaki inside a quantum computer. The garden is alive — but in a way that feels slightly alien. Bioluminescent flora has circuit-board patterning IN the leaves themselves. Architecture defies physics subtly. It's the most distinctive option — nobody has this style.

**Cedric treatment:** More symbolic/abstracted. Sometimes just a silhouette with glowing eyes. Sometimes rendered normally but standing in an impossible space. His robes have circuit-patterns woven in. He's less "guide in a garden" and more "guardian of a living algorithm."

**Circuit-root motif:** THE dominant visual element. Every surface is a hybrid — bark that IS circuitry, stone that IS a motherboard, water that flows through logic gates. The merger is complete, not decorative.

**Centre zone:** Abstract — gradients, particle fields, geometric grid fading to black. The centre feels like looking into a digital void where possibilities exist. Very clean for UI overlay but potentially disconnecting.

**Mood keywords:** `tech-organic surrealism, digital mysticism, bioluminescent circuit flora, impossible architecture, M.C. Escher botanical garden, quantum garden aesthetic, sacred geometry meets living systems`

**Who it appeals to:** Tech-forward audiences, AI enthusiasts, people who love cyberpunk and sci-fi. Extremely distinctive and shareable. Would go viral as an aesthetic.

**Risk:** Polarizing. Some people would LOVE it, some would find it "too weird" or "too cold." The surrealism might create distance instead of immersion for users who just want to find a startup idea. The "enchanted garden" warmth gets lost.

# 🔒 THE LOCKED RECOMMENDATION: STYLE B — "THE VERDANIA CODEX"

### Why this is the only right answer:

1. **Universal appeal ceiling:** Cinematic concept art reads as premium to literally everyone. Film has trained 8 billion people to respond to this visual language. Style A limits you to the "warm and whimsical" audience. Style C limits you to the "tech-forward" audience. Style B captures both.

2. **Cedric solves himself:** The biggest challenge is keeping Cedric consistent across 13 screens. Cinematic concept art gives you three escape hatches that other styles don't — dramatic rim-lighting silhouettes, three-quarter back views, and atmospheric haze softening. You can have Cedric recognisably present without needing pixel-perfect face matching.

3. **The centre zone is self-solving:** Volumetric haze and atmospheric depth mean the dark centre isn't "empty black" — it has life, light rays, subtle particle movement. Your UI floats in a *space*, not on a *surface*. This is the difference between "app with a pretty background" and "you are inside this world."

4. **The circuit-root motif becomes a genuine design signature:** In Style A it's too subtle to be memorable. In Style C it overwhelms. In Style B it's BOLD ENOUGH to be the thing people remember and screenshot, but INTEGRATED ENOUGH to feel like it belongs in this world. It's your visual brand — the thing that makes someone see a Catalst image and know it's Catalst before reading a word.

5. **It grows with the journey:** The dramatic lighting system gives you a natural emotional escalator. Gateway = cool moonlight. Inkblots = deep shadows. Forge = volcanic warmth. Ceremony = golden flood. Each screen FEELS different while being undeniably part of the same world. Style A and C can't modulate emotion through light this effectively.

6. **Mobile thumbnail test:** At Instagram Story size (where your founder card lives), Style B has the highest visual impact. Strong contrast, dramatic light, and bold colour accents read clearly at ANY size. Style A muddies, Style C confuses.

### CEDRIC ANGLE STRATEGIES (for character consistency):

When Midjourney struggles with faces, use these techniques:
- **Three-quarter back view:** Shows silhouette, hair, robes, posture — instantly recognisable without needing facial accuracy

- **Profile with atmospheric haze:** Side view with volumetric haze softening details — cinematic and forgiving
- **Engaged with object:** Cedric looking AT something (a crystal, a forge, a scroll) — face partially obscured naturally, body language carries identity

Every prompt includes this structural direction:

